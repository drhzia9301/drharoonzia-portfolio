About Me

Dr Haroon Zia

4th Year Medical Student

Hello! I'm Dr Haroon Zia, a dedicated and passionate 4th-year medical student at Saidu Medical College, Swat. My journey into medicine is driven by a profound desire to alleviate suffering and connect with patients on a human level.

Throughout my medical education, I've developed a keen interest in surgical specialties and medical innovation. I thrive in collaborative environments and am constantly seeking opportunities to learn and grow, both clinically and personally.

This website is my personal space to document my experiences, share my thoughts on various aspects of medicine and healthcare, and connect with fellow students, mentors, and anyone interested in the medical field. My goal is to become a compassionate and skilled Cardiologist.

Outside of my studies and clinical rotations, I enjoy reading and writing poetry.

My Medical Journey

2021 - Present

Medical School - Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS) Program

Saidu Medical College, Swat

Currently in my 4th year, I’m fully immersed in my clinical rotations, where I’ve especially enjoyed my time in both Medicine and Surgery. These experiences have helped me build essential hands-on skills and deepen my understanding of patient care and clinical decision-making.

Beyond the hospital and classroom, I stay actively involved in campus life — whether it’s participating in academic circles, mentoring peers, or contributing to student-led initiatives. For me, medical school isn’t just about studying — it’s about growing as a clinician, teammate, and future leader in healthcare.

2021 - Present (Concurrent with Medical School)

Records Head - The Blood Heroes Organization

I currently serve as the Records Head for The Blood Heroes Organization — a student-led initiative focused on bridging the gap between blood donors and patients in urgent need. In this role, I’m responsible for managing donor databases, streamlining communication, and coordinating efforts to ensure that life-saving support reaches those who need it most. It’s a position that has strengthened my organizational skills and deepened my commitment to community service.
